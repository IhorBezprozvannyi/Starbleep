About

Starbleep:Your window into active and historical planetary missions

Space agencies around the world collect and publish enormous amounts of data from their rovers and landers.

That data is public — but it's not always easy to find or understand.

Starbleep takes that raw information and turns it into something you can actually explore:

filter missions by type and status, see where rovers have been, track sensor readings over time, and get a clear picture of what each mission accomplished.

How to use

1. Choose a Planetary Body

Start on the home page and select whether you want to explore Mars or Moon missions. Each has its own set of rovers, landers, and orbiters.

2. Filter Missions

Use the filter to narrow down missions by type, status, and launch period. Missions appear as markers on a surface map of the planet.

3. Explore Mission Details

Click any mission marker to open its dedicated page — path data, sensor readings, and more, all in one place.
